The best defense is boring: **slow down**, verify payout rules, and refuse “too good to be true” funnels.

## Major red flags

- **Upfront payments** to unlock earnings or “premium withdrawals”
- **Crypto-only** payouts from unknown apps with no reputation
- **Endless ads** and fake progress bars that never cash out
- **No clear company identity** or support channel
- **Reviews** that look copied, bot-like, or all posted the same week

## Green flags (not guarantees, but better signals)

- Published terms, privacy policy, and support contact
- Known payout methods you recognize (PayPal, major gift cards)
- Years of history and discussion across independent communities

## If you already installed a suspicious app

- Remove permissions you do not need
- Do not enter banking logins into unknown WebViews
- Change passwords if you reused them

## FAQ

### Are all game “play to earn” apps scams?

Not all, but the category is higher risk. Treat low payouts as normal and huge promises as suspicious.

### Should I trust app store ratings alone?

No. Read recent reviews and look for payout complaints patterns.
