Most legit earn-on-your-phone options are **free to join**. The real cost is usually **time**, not a signup fee. If an app asks for money before you can withdraw, treat that as a major red flag.

## What “no deposit” should mean

- No payment required to create an account
- No “unlock withdrawals” fee
- Earnings come from ads, offers, surveys, or network use—not from you funding a wallet

## Categories that fit US users well

- **Surveys and GPT platforms**: Often free; payouts after you hit a minimum.
- **Passive bandwidth apps**: Free install; slow but hands-off.
- **Game reward apps**: Usually free; small payouts unless you grind offers.

## Quick safety checklist

- Read the cash-out rules before you invest hours
- Prefer known payout methods you already use
- Keep screenshots for high-effort offers

## FAQ

### Are free apps worth the time?

Sometimes, if you treat them as a side hobby with clear time limits.

### Why do some apps push paid upgrades?

That is different from “no deposit” earning. Stick to core free earning paths until you trust the platform.
